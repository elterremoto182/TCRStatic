## Best Practices for Using Temporary Power During Fire Recovery

Following these practices ensures safety and efficiency throughout restoration.

### Safety Checks and Initial Setup

Before setting up temporary power, evaluate the site for hazards:

- Check structural integrity
- Identify electrical risks
- Assess moisture levels
- Ensure proper grounding

**Always rely on certified pros for installation.** They will:

- Assess the site
- Determine best placement for equipment
- Handle necessary permits
- Evaluate power load requirements
- Double-check connections and post safety signs

Use [Ground Fault Circuit Interrupters](https://safeelectricity.org/ground-fault-circuit-interrupters-gfcis/) (GFCIs) to reduce shock risk. Keep temporary wiring shielded from damage and away from heavy equipment.

### Regular Monitoring and Maintenance

Once the system is running, regular monitoring is essential.

**Daily checks should include:**

- Signs of wear, overheating, or damage
- Fuel levels
- Secure cable connections
- Load requirements

**Prevent common problems:**

- Generator malfunctions
- Fuel shortages
- Overloaded circuits
- Damaged cables

Keep spare parts and fuel on hand. Train staff to spot early warning signs.

**Document everything** – fuel usage, operating hours, load measurements, and adjustments. These records help with insurance claims and compliance.

### Transitioning to Permanent Power

Switching from temporary to permanent power needs careful planning.

**Steps to follow:**

1. Work with licensed electricians to safely disconnect temporary systems
2. Inspect the permanent system
3. Confirm all repairs and upgrades are complete
4. Coordinate with your utility provider for phased reconnection
5. Get permits and inspections in advance
6. Test and approve the permanent system before removing temporary setup
7. Keep temporary power on standby until permanent power is fully operational
